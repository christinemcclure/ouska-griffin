Orthotics

Orthotics, or "arch supports" are an important treatment modality for aching, painful arches and heels.

What are orthotics? Simply put they are slender lightweight custom-made shoe inserts made out of carbon plastic blend materials to fit under arches to balance and support the foot. Our feet are durable but not invincible. Often as we age our feet weaken and our arches abnormally flatten or roll in. This is referred to as pronation. Pronation of the foot over many thousand steps in a day can cause soreness under the heel, arch, and up the ankle and leg.

Symptoms include pain with first steps in the morning or after rest, pain in heels during or after walking/running, tightness or burning to the knee cap or front shin bone after exercise. Diagnostic exam would include a thorough history, gait analysis, muscle and neurologic exam, and x-rays.

Fabrication of custom orthotics is as important as the evaluation. Construction begins with a distinct and unique non weight bearing plaster impression of your foot with your foot held in correct alignment by Dr. Ouska-Griffin. The orthotic shell is then molded from this custom impression at an outside lab.

Why not retail supports?
Mass produced over the counter supports will not fit nor relieve pain as well as a custom support made specifically for you. Likewise, foam cast impressions made with the patient standing in the foam only recreate the deformity and do not relieve pain. The skill is in the non weight bearing cast impression controlled by a experienced podiatrist. Orthotics are prescribed in the appropriate rigid or semi rigid materials depending on your pattern of exercise and sports activities. They can be worn in most athletic and walking shoes and with minor adjustments can be worn in some dress styles as well. Your foot is unique as are its needs.
