Walking for Fitness

Walking for fitness is a wonderful idea and an exercise choice much less stressful to our larger joints such as ankles knees and hips as we get older. Did you know that being only 10 pounds overweight increases force on the knees by 30 to 60 pounds with each step? Overweight women have nearly four times the risk of knee and foot arthritis and overweight men have nearly 5 times the risk of knee and foot arthritis.

Walking for fitness requires well fitting, supportive walking shoes. Age, fitness level, weight, and activity will dictate styles of shoes to pick from. Qualities of good walking shoes are:

  • Any supportive shoe will have a firm midsole. To test for midsole strength hold a shoe with both hands at heel and toe and try to compress the shoe. If it bends at the ball of the foot and not the midsole it has adequate support. If the shoe bends in the midsole it is not a well made shoe and should not be used for exercise.
  • At least 5 eye lace up.
  • Ample heel height(about 1.5 inches.) Also a quality shoe should have a firm heel counter, the back structure of any shoe that holds the heel of the foot.

If you have custom arch supports you should take them when shopping for shoes. The existing liner of the walking shoes should be removed to accommodate the arch supports.

If you chose to walk over running for fitness remember that to burn 200 calories per half hour you should be at a pace of at least a 20 minute mile, or 3 MPH. Gentle stretches prior to walking and more aggressive stretches after exercise help keep muscle and tendons from injury. Ankle and /or wrist weights can cause joint pain and are not recommended.
