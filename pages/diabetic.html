Diabetic Foot
Treatment of diabetic feet are a very important part of our practice. Why? Because for diabetics foot infections are all too common and potentially very serious. Diabetes not only raises our body’s blood sugar (glucose) levels but also causes poor circulation and nerve damage to feet. Did you know that the most common reason for diabetics to be admitted into the hospital is for a foot infection?

In most cases problems with diabetic feet can be prevented or minimized with a thorough medical evaluation and continuous treatment by a qualified podiatrist. Podiatrists have extensive training in caring for diabetic feet. We provide routine diabetic foot care of painful foot corns and callouses, treat ulcers, infections, and prescribe diabetic shoes with cushioned insoles. We offer diabetic socks designed to decease moisture and prevent blister formation. We provide prescriptions for Medicare diabetic shoes and specialized shoe modifications.


Diabetes can be diagnosed with a simple blood test. Those of us most likely to have diabetes will be

  1. Overweight with no daily exercise regimen
  2. Have a family history of diabetes
  3. Are 50 years old or older
  4. Have high blood pressure
  5. Have a history of high blood sugar during pregnancy

Diabetes can cause poor circulation in feet as well as nerve damage. Both of these complications are related to the level of sugar (glucose) measured in the blood stream. That is why good management of the daily blood sugar level is very important and should be monitored between your internist, endocrinologist, and podiatrist. Poor circulation can make any foot problem very complicated.

Proper care of diabetic feet require a special level of training and dedication and we provide that level in our practice.
